About Nirvana

Project Overview

  • "Nirvana" addresses stubble burning in Haryana by mapping stubble production, procuring crop residue, and processing it into ethanol. Utilizing satellite imagery and AI, abandoned lands are repurposed for storage. Fair prices incentivize farmers to participate, fostering sustainable ethanol production, environmental conservation, and economic prosperity.

Mission

  • Our mission is to combat stubble burning in Haryana by offering sustainable solutions. We aim to procure crop residue from farmers, process it into ethanol, and contribute to clean energy production. Through strategic planning and innovative technologies, we strive to mitigate environmental pollution and promote economic growth in the region.

The Ethanol Solution

Utilizing the Panipat 2G Ethanol Plant, our solution converts agricultural residue into ethanol, a sustainable energy source. Expected to yield 30 million liters annually, this initiative not only addresses air pollution and climate change but also promotes eco-friendly energy production. By transforming crop waste into valuable resources, we contribute to environmental conservation and sustainable development.
